    MAAT - Central Tejo

    5 MAAT - Central Tejo Belém e Ajuda Equipamentos 1900 – 1950 Vieillard e Touzet, 1908 vários autores,1951 12h00, 15h00 - Percurso Monumental; 18h00 - Percurso Secreto A unique building in the context of the architectural heritage of Lisbon, the Central Tejo power plant is one of the most beautiful examples of early 20th-century industrial architecture in Portugal. Its structure adopted the iron architectural style common in Western Europe at the time, and was given a solid brick cladding, which configures and decorates the façade in artistic styles ranging from Art Nouveau to Classicism. Today it is a museum that documents the history of electricity, with its original machinery totally intact. It also serves as a large exhibition space linked to its new neighbouring building.

    Gare Marítima da Rocha do Conde de Óbidos

    11 Gare Marítima da Rocha do Conde de Óbidos Belém e Ajuda Infraestruturas 1900 – 1950 Porfírio Pardal Monteiro, 1948 Ana Tostões This cruise terminal building is dominated by a large square-plan hall with a meticulous lighting design, which combines zenithal and lateral lights. It is a good example of how architects and engineers can and should work together. It also features two triptychs by Almada Negreiros, depicting aspects of everyday life in the vicinity of the dock – from an evocation of saltimbanques to Sunday walks along the river and the construction of the terminal buildings and to farewell scenes depicting the sadness of those leaving Lisbon and anxiety of those left.     Igreja de São Roque

    27 Igreja de São Roque Bairro Alto e Baixa-Chiado Equipamentos Até Séc. XVI Afonso Álvares e Filippo Terzi, Séc. XVI as visitas livres dão apenas acesso à igreja Filomena Brito e Maria Lino; nas visitas comentadas é visitado o forro do telhado The guided tours of this church dedicated to St. Roch include a visit behind the scenes and a walk over the roof of this iconic building. Built on the site of an old Manueline chapel, it is the work of the architect Afonso Álvares, but the roof and old Mannerist façade were designed by the Italian Filippo Terzi. A prototype for Portuguese Jesuit churches, it belonged to the Society of Jesus before it was donated to the Santa Casa da Misericórdia charity in 1768 after the Jesuits were expelled from Portugal. This renovation project set out to completely remodel the apartment, adapting it to contemporary living and reinstating the original interior relationships by restoring the original balconies over the gardens that characterised the urban plan for this estate. The interior is now defined and structured by a new body – a line of closets that organises and provides support to the dwelling’s various spaces (storage space was a fundamental aspect of the new programme).
